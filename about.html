Patrick Loewen was in kitchens since he was a young boy. From his first shifts cutting chips,
in an Irish boarding school,where his Canadian Mother had been unable to maintain boarding fees,
he knew he had found a home. His childhood had been rocky, and it was in the kitchens of Ireland,
and Western Canada that he grew up. A brief stint at the Emily Carr, School of Art in Vancouver,
is reflected in his use of colour, and his philosophies about food and human culture, are deeply
rooted in his work, and the people around him.
"The culinary arts, are the greatest arts in my opinion, because they capture all five senses, and
it is an activity shared across the entire spectrum of humanity. The very act of walking upright was
caused by our adaptive diet at the end of the ice age. Meals are tied to memories, and history. They
celebrate occasions and traditions. To have control, even in just the slightest way, of such a powerful
force, is exhilirating." - Patrick Loewen
Patrick began cooking in the West of Ireland as a teenager, after boarding school. He moved home
to Canada at ninteen years old, and began working as a potwasher under Corey Pelan. He completed his
aprentiship, and moved to the Coast Hotel in Victoria, British Columbia, where they catered to such events
as G8 Summits, and Hollywood cast wrap up Parties. He then went to the Hotel Grand Pacific, at the time
Canada's #1 5* Hotel, and assisted catering in such high scale events as the Emporor of Japan's visit to
the Governor Generals Mansion. He then relocatted to the Inn at Laurel Point, to work in both pastries
and savories, under the leadership of Bradd Horen, the Captain of the Canadian National Culinary Team.
He assisted in traing with the team, and finished his pastry diploma program. This hotel was the only
hotel of that scale in Western Canada to make all of its own breads and pastries, from breakfast croissants,
to burger buns. It was shortly after this hotel, that Patrick and his wife opened their own bakery which
was soon after purchased from them for a lucrative proffit. With this money he moved back to Ireland, with
his wife and two baby boys. He brought with him a sense of adventure found in Western Canadian cuisine, and
settled in Dublin, eventually working as a sous chef for Irish Master Chef host, Dylan McGragh before moving
to Galway, where he head chef'd one of the countries most prestigious seafood restaurants. He has cooked
competitivley for over a decade, and has a list of medals and achievments. He is an acredited chef, pastry chef,
and butcher. He has a deep knowledge of the industry, in forms of creativity, marketing, efficincy, and financials.
His understanding of food, service, and motivation are surpassed only by his
passion for them.
